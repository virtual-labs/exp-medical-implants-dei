Artificial Tooth Forming Simulation

Description: This simulation demonstrates the manufacturing of an artificial tooth using Equal Channel Angular Pressing (ECAP), a severe plastic deformation technique. A titanium alloy billet (10 mm diameter, 100 mm length) is processed through a 120° ECAP die at room temperature. A small portion of the processed billet is cut and forged into tooth-shaped dies to obtain the final shape. The crown portion is then formed using another die. The complete artificial tooth with flash and all manufacturing steps are shown in this simulation.

Artificial Elbow Joint Simulation

Description: This simulation shows the manufacturing of an artificial elbow joint using ECAP processed titanium alloy. A billet (25 mm diameter, 200 mm length) is processed through a 120° ECAP die. The processed billet is cut into parts and shaped through multiple forging operations including die forging and binding. The final assembly and exploded view of the elbow joint are displayed, illustrating each stage of the manufacturing process.

Artificial Hip Joint Simulation

Description: This simulation demonstrates the manufacturing of an artificial hip joint using ECAP processed titanium alloy. A billet (25 mm diameter, 200 mm length) is first processed through a 120° ECAP die. The processed billet is then placed between specially designed hydraulic hip-shaped dies to form the final artificial hip joint. The simulation shows the transformation process and the final forged hip joint with flash.
